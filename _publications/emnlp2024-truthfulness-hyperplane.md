Abstract
-------
We discover that there exists a universal truthfulness hyperplane inside the intermediate hidden representations of large language models, which acts as a general control direction that can effectively enhance or suppress the factuality of model outputs across different tasks, datasets, and model families. This finding provides novel interpretability perspectives for understanding and improving the truthfulness of large language models.
